Personas & Storyboarding

1.1 Project Context

I chose to investigate a public interface to understand its intended functions and user experience, through observation and interviews of actual users.

1.2 Interface: Ratty Drinking Machine

2.1 Observations

I observed three users interacting with the interface of Ratty Drinking Machine, and here are some of my findings.

Observation Description
One Iteration on the Drinking Machine Takes 15 Seconds

The three interviewees A, B, and C each spent 17, 26, and 14 seconds using the machine, where B ran two iterations. Therefore, one iteration takes an average of 15 seconds to complete.
Hesitation Correlates with Availability of Choices

  • A spent 8 out of 13 seconds on the drink page, where multiple flavors were present.

  • B spent 20 out of 26 seconds on the iteration where he got lemonade, which came with over 10 choices. He only spent 6 seconds on getting water that has a single option.
Smooth Outlet Usage

All three interviewees used ice and drink outlets with no visible hesitation or difficulty.
Soft Drink Dominates

Interviewees each got root beer, lemonade, and coca cola. No flavored water or water was obtained for drinking purpose.

2.2 Interview

I interviewed the observed users after they finished using the drinking machine, and below are the interview questions and a summary of responses.

2.2.1 Interview Questions

  1. Why do you use the drinking machine?
  2. What drink(s) do you get most often?
  3. What is your expectation for the process of operating the machine?
  4. What decisions did you make before coming to the machine?
  5. What decisions did you make while using the machine?
  6. What do you think of the grouping of the logos?
  7. What do you think of the placement of the “water” button?
  8. Is darkening the color a good indicator of the drink is gone?
  9. What do you think of the filter categories?
  10. Is everything on the home screen accessible and legible?
  11. Do you find the calorie information on the drink screen helpful?
  12. Is the presentation of different flavors on the drink screen clear?
  13. Is the outlet design intuitive?
  14. What hurdles did you face when using the machine, if any?
  15. What additional functions would you like to add to the machine?
  16. Describe your idea process of using the machine.

2.2.2 Interview Response Summary

  • Obvious "Water" Button on Home Screen:

    All interviewees expected easy access to hydration, and they most frequently get water. They were satisfied with the standalone "water" button, though interviewee A suggested that making water the default option would be ideal.

  • Grouping of the Drinks on Home Screen:

    All interviewees chose drink and flavor while using the machine. The clear organization of the home screen helps the decision-making process, as it places the most common beverage choice - soda - in the middle. However, interviewee C found some side groups confusing, and that adding a title to each group indicating the type of drinks would be helpful.

  • Inconspicuous Filter Options on Home Screen:

    None of the interviewees noticed the filter options at the bottom of the home screen, and they suggested a better presentation of those layouts would be helpful.

  • 2 Main Sources of Frustration:

    • Drink wanted was gone

      • Interviewee B suggested that different drinking machines in the same dining hall should communicate and indicate if a drink is available in another machine.
      • Interviewee C complained that a drink logo stays colored even if some flavors are gone.

    • Couldn’t Customize the Flavor

      • Interviewee A hoped to choose to mix drinks with one iteration.
      • Interviewee B hoped to choose dilution levels (mix drink and water) with one iteration.

3.1 Personas

Based on the observation and interviews, I created two personas to represent users of Ratty Drinking Machine.

3.1.1 Exhausted John

Problem
  • Confusing and unclear grouping of some drinks on the home page.
  • The low-calorie filter is not very obvious. John didn't know such option exists.
  • Many drinks are unavailable and it's unclear if they might be available at other machines.
  • John has to operate the machine twice to mix two drinks.
Representation
  • John represents the users of the Ratty drinking machine as all the problems he faces are faced by actual users (interviewees).
  • John's actions correponds to interviewees' actions
  • John’s identity as a student-athlete comes from practice coincides with some of the interviewees.

3.1.2 Busy Sasha

Problem
  • Home screen doesn't necessarily reflect availability of particular flavors.
  • Sasha has to operate the machine twice to dilute drink.
Representation
  • Sasha represents the users of the Ratty drinking machine as all the problems he faces are faced by actual users (interviewees).
  • Sasha's actions correponds to some interviewees' actions
  • Sasha’s situation (under a time crunch for classes) coincides with some interviewees.
